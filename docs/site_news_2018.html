20-22 October 2018

A new page name: Amanita sp-59 has become Amanita pseudopenetrans. One of the reasons for using this name is that, macroscopically, it seems to have some affinity to species of stirps Penetratrix; however, genetically it is quite distinct from the Penetratrices and, indeed, from all species of section Vaginatae that, to date, have sequences posted in GenBank.

28 May 2018

New stirps and taxonomic moves: The stirps Ovoidea is created and contains Amanita neoovoidea, A. ovoidea, and A. proxima.  This stirps is segregated from section Amidella and is placed in section Lepidella. This movement is justifiable on morphological and molecular grounds. This also results in A. proxima, which contains allenic norleucine being placed in the same section with stirps Rhopalopus the three species of which also contain the nephrotoxin.
