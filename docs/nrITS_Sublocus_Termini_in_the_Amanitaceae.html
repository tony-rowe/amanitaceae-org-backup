Nuclear Ribosomal Internal Transcribed Spacer Sublocus Termini
in the Amanitaceae

Version 24 - 22 April 2016 
This page records the observed terminal motifs for the 3' end of the 18S rDNA locus, the 5' and 3' ends of the 5.8S rDNA locus, and the 5' end of the 28S rDNA locus.  In Amanita these terminal motifs often have a few adjacent bases [in either ITS1 or ITS2] that appear to be somewhat stable within one or more sections of the genus.  Using the sequences that we have certified as based on vouchers revised in either the Yang or Tulloss laboratories, we have explored:
  • the rDNA terminal motifs

  • the terminal sequences of ITS1 and ITS2
On this page we catalog our findings.

Below there is a drawing showing the order of the components in fungal nuclear ribosomal DNA.  The drawing is followed by outlined blocks providing examples of the (relatively) conserved terminal motifs of the 18S, 5.8S, and 28S segments (pale purple background in the outlined blocks) and the adjacent sequences of bases found at the termini of the ITS1 and ITS2 segments (black background in the outlined blocks).
