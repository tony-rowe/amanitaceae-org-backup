A confusion eliminated: The confusion regarding the section to which Amanita sp-N58 should be assigned is resolved.  The species appears to belong to stirps Strobilaceovolvata in section Vaginatae.

Although, the single collection of A. sp-N49 (made on the same foray as the initial collection of A. sp-N58 and also assignable to the provisional series Penetratrices) did not have a partial veil when collected, what we know of the two temporarily coded organisms suggest that they are very similar and might be a single taxon.

10-14 June 2019

New taxa: Amanita scutum is proposed as the name of an apparently new taxon with three color zones on the cap having a dark-light-dark ordering and the less common nrLSU 5' motif seen in A. penetratrix and other recently proposed species of the Vaginatae.  Two new species of section Phalloideae have been described from Africa—A. bweyeyensis and A. harkoneniana.

Updated brief tabs: Amanita maryaliceae and A. ostendemihi.

Updated techtabs: Amanita bweyeyensis, A. harkoneniana, Amanita maryaliceae, A. ostendemihi, A. scutum, and Amanita sp-GSM02.

5-9 June 2019

New taxa: Amanita petroleum is a newly proposed taxon from the Gulf Coast states of the U.S. with a very black cap that can be quite viscid when wet.  It can be mistaken for A. anthracina in the field.  We are just beginning to learn about it, and a new page has been established for it. Amanita pseudoinsinuans is another, new "ringed ringless amanita." Its cap is distinctly more brown than that of insinuans.  Its volva appears to become gray and friable. These taxa were found by Logan Wiedenfeld and were distinguished molecularly using data from Stephen Russell and colleagues at Purdue.  The temporary code name "Amanita sp-MO02" is replaced by a provisional name A. ostendemihi."

Updated brief tabs: Amanita ostendemihi, A. petroleum, and A. pseudoinsinuans.

Updated techtabs: Amanita ostendemihi, A. petroleum, A. pseudoinsinuans, and A. sp-C22.
